Pg 46: "Not at all.What I want to examine is how the processes of imperialism occurred beyond the level of economic laws and political decisions, and—by predisposition, by the authority of recognizable cultural formations, by continuing consolidation within education, literature, and the visual and musical arts—were manifested at another very significant level, that of the national culture, which we have tended to sanitize as a realm of unchanging intellectual monuments, free from worldly affiliations."

About

The role and impact of Literature throughout history has always been large as literature has been present in all types of civilisations through art, songs, poetry, storytelling, visual and musical arts, etc.

The aim of this website it to create a unique and simple explanation of the historical relationship between Empire and Literature.

Our goals are to enlighten, educate and pique the interest of viewers/users on how literature contributed to the expansion of colonialism through the historical relationship between Empire and Literature. We achieved this goal by looking at Jane Austin's novel Mansfield Park and how the novel can be linked to the relationship and expansion.
